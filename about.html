Play Africa

Play africa is Southern Africa's first dedicated children's museum. We are located at Constitutional Hill in Braamfontein, Johannesburg. Constitution Hill is a historic site that was once a prison and military fort. Today it is home to South Africa's Constitutional Court and stands as a symbol of justice and democracy

Play Africa opened its doors to bring joy, learning, and creativity to this powerful space. We believe that every child deserves the right to play, explore, and discover the world around them.

Our Mission

To empower children with 21st-century skills through curiosty, creativity, and play - building a new generation ready to shape Africa's future.

Our Values

  • Play - We believe play is how children learn best.
  • Inclusion - We welcome all children regardless of background.
  • Creativity - We encouraging imagination and innovation.
  • Community - We work with families, schools, and partners.
  • Heritage - We honour South Africa's history and culture.

Our history

Play Africa was founded with the vision of creating a safe, fun, and educational space for children in Johannesburg. Starting as a small initiative, it has grown into a nationally recognised children's museum that serves thousands of learners through school trips, community programmes, and family visits.

In 2025, Play Africa was honoured to join the Moleskine Foundation's Creativity Pioneers Fund, recognising our contribution to creative education across Africa.

Who we serve

  • Children aged 3 to 12 years
  • School groups and educators
  • Families and Parents
  • Community organisations

Our Partners

Play Africa works with local and international partners including the Moleskine Foundation, the Canada Fund for local initiatives (CFLI), and various South African government and education bodies.
